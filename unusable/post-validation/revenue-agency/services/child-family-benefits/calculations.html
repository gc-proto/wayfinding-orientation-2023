Canada child benefit (CCB) calculations

How are CCB payments calculated

Every month, your CCB payment is adjusted based on:

  • the number of children in your care
  • the age of your children
  • your marital status
  • your net family income from the previous year
Estimate your CCB payment

Maximum CCB payment

If your family income is under $31,120, the maximum CCB payment for July 2019 to June 2020 is for each eligible child:

  • under 6: $6,639 per year ($553.25 per month)
  • aged 6 to 17: $5,602 per year ($466.83 per month)

Your CCB payment gradually starts decreasing when your family net income goes over $31,120.

  • Definition of net family income

    Your adjusted net family income is:

    • your net family income (line 236 of your tax return, plus line 236 of your spouse's or common-law partner's tax return)
    • minus any universal child care benefit (UCCB) and registered disability savings plan (RDSP) income received (line 117 and line 125 of your or your spouse's tax return)
    • plus any UCCB and RDSP amounts repaid (line 213 and line 232 of your or your spouse's tax return)

Your payment is based on your income

The reduction of the maximum benefit depends on your income and on the number of children for which you receive CCB.

For how many eligible children?

  • 1
  • 2
  • 3
  • 4 or more

If you have 1 child

  • Family income below $31,120

    Your get the maximum CCB payment for that child, and it is not reduced. For each eligible child:

    • under 6: $6,639 per year ($553.25 per month)
    • aged 6 to 17: $5,602 per year ($466.83 per month)
  • Family income over $31,120 but less than $67,427

    If you have 1 child, your total CCB payment is reduced by:

    • 7% of your income over $31,120

    Martha would receive $5,667.40 ($472.28 per month) in CCB payments, for the July 2019 to June 2020 period.
