Residency status (required)
Are you still a resident of Canada for income tax purposes? (required)

You might be a resident of Canada for tax purposes if you are one of the following:

  • A factual resident of Canada: you keep significant residential ties in Canada while living or travelling outside the country.
  • A deemed resident of Canada: you are in one of the following situations:
    1. You lived outside Canada during the tax year, did not have significant residential ties, and you are a government employee, a member of the Canadian Forces including their overseas school staff, or working under a Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA) assistance program.
    2. This could also apply to the family members of an individual who is in one of these situations.
    3. You sojourned in Canada for 183 days or more in the tax year, do not have significant residential ties with Canada, and are not considered a resident of another country under the terms of a tax treaty between Canada and that country.
Province/territory of residence (required)

Select the last province or territory you lived in before leaving Canada.

You do not qualify for Canadian tax benefits

As you are not a resident of Canada for tax purposes, you don't qualify for any of the tax benefits or credits.
