Canada Child Benefit: How much you can expect

  • Overview
  • Eligibility
  • Apply
  • How much you can expect
  • Payment dates

Your Canada child benefit (CCB) payment is recalculated every July based on your family net income from the previous year. Make sure your personal information is up to date so that you always get the right payment.

The maximum you can receive is:

  • $6,496 per year ($541.33 per month) for each eligible child under the age of six
  • $5,481 per year ($456.75 per month) for each eligible child aged 6 to 17

Your CCB payment gradually starts decreasing when your family net income goes over $30,450. If you are already eligible for the CCB, go to My Account or mobile apps to discover how much you will receive.

Calculator

You can also use the online child and family benefits calculator to get an estimate of how much you will receive.

Calculate your CCB payment

Keep your information up to date

Make sure you keep your information up to date. This will help avoid situations where you are getting less money than you should or required to pay back sums for which you are not eligible to receive.

Use CRA My Account to update your personal information in the following situations:

  • your marital status changes
  • you change your address
  • you want to start, change or stop direct deposit
  • the number of children in your care changes
  • you start or stop sharing custody with another individual of one or more children
  • a death has occurred
